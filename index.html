Marijuana- How the States Stack Up

Introduction

Marijuana Use Among People 12 and Older in the United States, by Geographic Characteristics. Data comes from the Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA) Credible Source, supported by The White House, Department of Health and Human Services, and the US Government.This experiment was conducted over an 8 year timespan, from 2004 and 2012.The data set contains appropriate information for us, allowing us to draw reasonable conclusions.There are 3 age groups within the data : 12-17, 18-25, and Over 25.

Abstract

With changing policies and laws about marijuana use in the United States, the usage of marijuana for medical and recreational purposes has risen over time. Given this information, exactly what percentage of the United States population uses marijuana? How does this change when considering different demographics separated by age or location? Understanding trends in marijuana use within different demographics allows for more informed usage regulations, such as whether marijuana should only be allowed for certain age groups, and other related policies. The analysis performed revealed which age group used the most marijuana as well as which geographic region used the most marijuana in a given year, and how those trends changed over time.

Visualizations

The goal of this article is to analyze trends in marijuana usage among different age groups and regions in the United States. It also will help to establish the marijuana usage trends throughout the country, and help to determine which region in the United States consumed the most Marijuana and what age group consumed the most Marijuana in a specific region. Through this article we will be able to determine whether the age group for Marijuana usage should be increased in the areas where Marijuana is legal. Also, we will determine where the most Marijuana consumption is and see whether that depends upon their laws and enforcement policies etc. Anyway we will find the region and age group where Marijuana consumption is the most and least. The three different age groups examined in the study were ages 12-17, 17-25, and older than 25. When the data for each of these age groups were analyzed, it allowed us to draw conclusions about marijuana use among teenagers and how it differs from marijuana use among younger and older adults. There were 6 distinct regions of the United States that were examined in the study: the Northeast, East, North Central, Northwest, South Central, and Southwest. When the data for each of these regions were analyzed, it allowed us to compare how marijuana usage differs based on where people live. The study examined three different levels of historical usage among participants in the survey: People who smoked pot for the first time in their life, people who had used marijuana in the last month, and people who had used marijuana in the last year. The analysis allowed us to see how usage trends changed over time, and to see how the Marijuana usage varied across the regions of the United States and what group consumed the most Marijuana.
